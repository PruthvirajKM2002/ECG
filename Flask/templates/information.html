Classification Guide

Normal

The heart beats regularly, with an orderly sequence of electrical signals causing the atria (upper chambers) to contract followed by the ventricles (lower chambers), resulting in effective blood pumping.
P wave: Contraction of the atria.
QRS complex: Contraction of the ventricles.
T wave: Relaxation of the ventricles.

Ventricular Fibrillation

This is a chaotic, irregular heart rhythm originating from the ventricles. It's a medical emergency as it can lead to cardiac arrest and death within minutes if not treated promptly. The PQRST waveform is chaotic with no discernible pattern.

Premature Atrial Contraction

These are extra, abnormal heartbeats originating in the atria. They often occur due to stress, caffeine, or other stimulants. The PQRST waveform may have an early P wave followed by a normal QRS complex and T wave.

Premature Ventricular Contraction

These are extra, abnormal heartbeats originating in the ventricles. They can be benign or indicate underlying heart problems. The PQRST waveform may show a wide and bizarre QRS complex occurring earlier than expected.

Right Bundle Branch Block

This is a delay or blockage in the electrical signals along the right bundle branch, which can be due to heart disease or other factors. The QRS complex in the PQRST waveform is widened and may appear slurred.

Left Bundle Branch Block

Similar to RBBB but affecting the left bundle branch. It can indicate heart disease and may lead to abnormal heart rhythms. The QRS complex in the PQRST waveform is widened, often with a broad and notched appearance.
